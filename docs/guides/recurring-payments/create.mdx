FlowGuard supports recurring payouts in two product surfaces:

- the dedicated **Payments** workflow
- the shared **Streams** workflow using recurring templates

Use the dedicated payment flow when you want a straightforward fixed-cadence payout. Use the shared stream builder when you want recurring schedules to live alongside other treasury stream families and batch payroll tools.

## In the App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the recurring payment flow">
    Go to **Payments -> Create Payment** for the dedicated payment workflow, or use a recurring template inside **Streams -> Create Stream** if you want the shared stream builder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the recipient and cadence">
    Choose the recipient, asset, amount per interval, and cadence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the recurring mode">
    Pick either:

    - a fixed recurring schedule with a bounded duration
    - a refillable recurring runway if you want an open-ended treasury payroll lane

  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the schedule preview">
    The app shows the cadence and payout structure before creation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create and fund">
    Create the recurring payment or recurring stream, then sign the funding transaction from the wallet.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to Decide Up Front

| Decision            | Why it matters                                                      |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Recipient           | recurring contracts do not support recipient transfer later         |
| Cadence             | the contract enforces the interval you choose                       |
| Fixed vs refillable | refillable recurring streams can be extended without redeploying    |
| BCH vs CashTokens   | affects funding and display, but the recurring model stays the same |

## Best Fit

- **Dedicated payment flow**: subscriptions, simple payroll, retainers
- **Recurring stream in the shared stream builder**: treasury payroll lanes, batch payroll, organization-linked recurring schedules
